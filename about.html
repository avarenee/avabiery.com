About me

Born and raised in rural Ohio, I moved to Middletown, Connecticut to study my first and most enduring love: Mathematics.

I've shared this love with my community by tutoring Middletown public school children, as well as by working for Wesleyan's Mathematics and Computer Science department as a tutor and course assistant.

I have a passion for libraries, and have worked as a circulation assistant for Olin Library, as well as assisted in program development with Russell Library during my semester as Community Service Chair for my chapter of the Alpha Delta Phi literary society.

I also have a number of creative interests, including theater and writing. In 2018, I performed the role of Emily in a Wesleyan student production of the 2013 play Disgraced by Ayad Akhtar. For the four years I attended Wesleyan, I co-organized and performed in a production called ESQUE, a student showcase founded to uplift the creative voices of marginalized students. I write poetry and short stories in my free time.

Currently, I'm working on my development portfolio. Recent projects of mine include RESP, a progressive web app for use in natural disaster relief, and MetaCleanup, a Python command-line tool to fix music library metadata. More about these projects here. I'm also actively submitting my poetry to literary magazines. Excerpts of my writings can be found here.
